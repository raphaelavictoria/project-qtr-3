FIRE PREVENTION

Fire safety is important and necessary in the workplace in order to prevent and protect against the destruction caused by fire. Fire safety reduces the risk of injury and building damage that fires can cause. Developing and implementing fire safety protocols in the workplace is not only required by law but it is crucial to everyoneâ€™s safety that may be in the building during a fire emergency.

Fire Safety Tips

Test smoke alarms

 If they aren't working, change the batteries as it may result to overheating. Checking them from time to time ensures they are functioning properly. It plays an essential part in warning family members of smoke detection inside your homes.

Have a fire escape plan

 Because fires are unpredictable, create a fire escape plan and make sure everyone in your home is aware of it. Practice it regularly, and designate a meeting spot outside of your home.

Be careful when smoking

 If you smoke, do it outside, and dispose of cigarettes properly. Do not smoke indoors and make sure all smoking materials are fully extinguished.

Don't Overload Outlets

 According to the Consumer Product Safety Commission, overloaded circuits and outlets cause more than 5,000 residential fires each year. Exceeding the rated load for the circuit wiring causes the circuit breaker to trip, shutting off the power to the entire circuit.

Have Fire Extinguishers

 Make sure they are placed in areas where fires are more likely to occur, keep them in places where it's easy to access. Its efficient to have one since extinguishers were capable of extinguishing fires approximately 95% of the time.
